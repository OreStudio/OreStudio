#+description: When the two-pass title ranker strips common question verbs (create, open, add, …) to extract the core query, expand each stripped verb with its synonyms in the full-pass FTS query so docs whose titles use a synonym still score a full-pass title hit. E.g. 'how do i create a sprint' expands 'create' to {open, start, scaffold, make} so 'How do I open a new sprint?' gets a title match even though 'create' != 'open'. The synonym table lives statically in compass.py.

* Goal

Improve =compass search= recall for natural-language questions by making
the full-pass title ranker verb-synonym-aware.  When the user queries
"how do I create a sprint?", docs titled "How do I open a new sprint?"
now score a full-pass title hit even though "create" ≠ "open" — because
the full-pass FTS expression OR-groups =create= with its cluster synonyms
(=open=, =start=, =make=, =build=, …).

* Acceptance

- =QueryPlan.full_fts_expr= is a pre-built SQLite FTS5 expression where
  each question verb is expanded into an OR group containing all members
  of its synonym cluster; non-verb content words remain plain AND terms.
- The full-pass title rank in =cmd_search= uses =full_fts_expr= directly
  rather than AND-joining =full_words=.
- A keyword-only query (no question verbs) produces a plain AND expression
  identical to the old behaviour — no regression.
- =QUESTION_VERB_SYNONYMS= (in =search_scorer.py=) is the single source of
  truth for verb clusters; bidirectional membership is enforced by construction.

* Plan

Added =QUESTION_VERB_SYNONYMS: dict[str, frozenset[str]]= to
=search_scorer.py=, built from six verb clusters.  Added =full_fts_expr:
str= field to =QueryPlan=; =from_query= builds it by iterating =tokens=
and replacing each question verb with an OR group of its cluster synonyms.

Updated =_title_fts_ranks()= in =compass.py= to accept an optional
=fts_expr= string; when supplied it is used verbatim instead of joining
=word_list=.  The full-pass call passes =fts_expr=_qplan.full_fts_expr=.
