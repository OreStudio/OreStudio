* Goal

Give IR indices the same data-driven display-convention story FX
already has. =currency_pair_convention= carries =pip_factor=,
=tick_size= and =decimal_places= per pair because quoting precision
genuinely varies (JPY crosses at 2dp, EUR/USD at 4dp) — it is not
hardcoded in the UI. IR has no equivalent: =overnight_index_convention=
only holds fixing calendar/day-count/settlement-lag, and
=floating_index_type= is bare id+description.

This task:
- Researches how real trading systems actually quote IR rates: percent
  vs basis points, and typical decimal precision by currency/index
  tier (G10 overnight RFRs vs EM curves), rather than assuming one
  convention fits all.
- Adds the equivalent convention field(s) to =floating_index_type= (or
  a new =ir_index_convention= entity, whichever the research points
  to) capturing that precision/unit choice per index.
- Wires a shared Qt formatter so any rate-displaying surface (starting
  with =curve-snapshot-builder-viewer='s Grid/History tabs) consumes
  it instead of hardcoding a format.

Raised while designing curve-snapshot-builder-viewer's Grid tab —
deliberately split out rather than solved inline there, since it is a
genuine reference-data question, not a UI-only concern.
