#+title: Editing a business unit doesn't live-refresh Org Explorer
#+description: Saving a business unit edit from the Business Units window doesn't trigger Org Explorer's tree to refresh/reflect the change -- missing cross-component eventing wiring.
#+type: capture
#+level: cross
#+filetags: :qt:trading:refdata:eventing:
#+created: 2026-07-18
#+updated: 2026-07-18

This page is a [[id:671F18E4-E09C-4B3B-BD24-D33DF8AE38A6][capture]] in the *inbox* bucket of the product backlog — a pre-sprint idea, not yet pulled into a sprint as a story.

* What

=OrgExplorerMdiWindow= has a manual *Reload* toolbar button
(=EntityListMdiWindow::reload= wired at construction) but no
subscription to =business_unit_changed_event= (or the equivalent for
book/trade) — grepped the source for =notifyOpenDialogs=/
=BusinessUnitChanged=/any reload-on-event pattern and found none.
Editing a business unit from the standalone Business Units window
therefore leaves an already-open Org Explorer showing stale data
until the user manually clicks Reload.

* Why

Every other generated list window auto-refreshes on its own entity's
change event (the standard =*_changed_event= NATS subscription
codegen wires in); Org Explorer, being hand-written and aggregating
three entity types into one tree, doesn't get this for free and
appears to have never had it added by hand either.

* References

- =projects/ores.qt/trading/src/OrgExplorerMdiWindow.cpp= — no event
  subscription found; =doReload()= is the only refresh path.
