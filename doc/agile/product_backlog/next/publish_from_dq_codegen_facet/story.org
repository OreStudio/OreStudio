* Goal

Add a new =ores.codegen= facet (provisionally =publish_from_dq=) that,
from a single declaration on an entity's existing codegen model,
generates the mechanical parts of the DQ "publish-from-dq" pattern:
the artefact-type registration row, a shadow-table model *derived*
from the entity's own model (removing today's hand-duplicated
=ores.codegen.lookup_entity= file), the =SECURITY DEFINER=
=ores_<service>_publish_<entity>_from_dq_fn= SQL function, the NATS
=publish_from_dq_handler=, and its registrar wiring. See
[[id:F05B779F-769B-49D5-B369-D2AAC029B36D][DQ Bundle Publication Pipeline]] — specifically its worked example
(=report_definitions= in the =ore_analytics= bundle) and codegen-value-
assessment table — for the concrete pattern to generalise from and the
full rationale for why this is the one part of the bundle-publish
pipeline with no codegen support today, hand-written 25 times across
=ores.refdata=, =ores.assets=, and =ores.reporting=.

This story was split out of
[[id:E5EC80BB-789B-4BD6-B6E5-DC7AA790692A][Synthetic data librarian support: FX foundation]] (sprint 22), which
needed exactly this facet but, on reflection, the codegen work is
substantial enough to deserve its own dedicated story rather than
blocking that story's delivery. The FX story hand-writes its
publish-from-dq function for now, using =report_definitions= as the
template to copy.

* Acceptance

- Enabling the facet on an entity's codegen model generates a working
  artefact-type row, shadow table, publish function, handler, and
  registrar entry — no hand-written SQL/C++ beyond the declaration.
- Facet correctly parameterises =mode= (upsert/insert_only/replace_all)
  and natural-key matching, since these vary slightly across the 25
  existing hand-written functions.
- Regenerating at least one existing hand-written instance (e.g.
  =report_definitions=, and ideally the FX publish function
  hand-written in the meantime) with the facet produces a semantic
  zero-diff against the hand-written version, which can then be
  retired.
- The hand-authored =ores.codegen.lookup_entity= shadow-table models
  are retired in favour of the facet deriving shape from the target
  entity's own model.

* Decisions

- Pre-existing bug found while hand-writing the FX story's shadow
  artefact table: =ores.codegen='s enrichment step that populates the
  mustache =entity= context (needed to render
  =sql_schema_artefact_create.mustache=) is gated on
  =model_type == 'schema'=, but =ores.codegen.lookup_entity= models map
  to internal model_type =enum=. So =sql_schema_artefact_create= cannot
  render from *any* =lookup_entity= model today, including the
  pre-existing =ores.dq.report_definition_lookup_entity.org= — this
  isn't new breakage from this story, it means the shadow-table
  generation path has been silently broken/unused. Fix this as part of
  (or as a prerequisite to) this facet's implementation.
