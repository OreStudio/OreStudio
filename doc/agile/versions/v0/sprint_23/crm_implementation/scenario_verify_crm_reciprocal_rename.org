** Confirm the toggle reads "Show Reciprocal"

The footer button that used to read "Show Inverted" now reads *Show
Reciprocal*; hover it and confirm the tooltip text also says
"reciprocal" rather than "inverted".

*** Result

| Field  | Value |
|--------+-------|
| Status | PASS |

** Toggle "Show Reciprocal" on and check the backfilled cells

With the toggle off, note a cell with no direct quote shows a dash.
Turn "Show Reciprocal" on and confirm that cell now shows a
pastel-tinted computed 1/rate value instead of a dash, and that its
sparkline/history still populates correctly.

*** Result

| Field  | Value |
|--------+-------|
| Status | PASS |

** Toggle "Show Reciprocal" off and confirm the cell reverts

Turn the toggle back off; the previously-backfilled cell should
return to showing a dash (no direct quote), confirming the toggle
still round-trips correctly.

*** Result

| Field  | Value |
|--------+-------|
| Status | PASS |

** Export CSV with "Show Reciprocal" on and check the reciprocal column

With the toggle on, use the matrix's CSV export. Open the exported
file and confirm:
- its filename reflects "reciprocal" rather than "inverted" (matching
  the renamed filter state);
- the header row now includes a =reciprocal= column
  (=base,quote,rate,status,reciprocal,as_of=), and every backfilled
  row shows =true= there, every directly-quoted row shows =false=.

** Check the shell's =--reciprocal= flag

From =ores.shell=, run =crm rates <crm-name> --reciprocal= for the
same CRM used above and confirm the output includes the same
backfilled pairs seen in the Qt matrix, with rates matching. Compare
against =crm rates <crm-name>= (no flag) to confirm the flag is what
toggles the backfill, same as the Qt "Show Reciprocal" button.

*** Result

| Field  | Value |
|--------+-------|
| Status | PENDING |

** Confirm Live/Stale/Disconnected status colouring and tooltips

For a rate that is currently ticking, hover its cell and confirm the
tooltip reads "Live - <timestamp>" and the pair-code text is neutral
(no colour tint). Stop the underlying synthetic feed (Market
Simulator window) for one pair in the CRM, wait past the 15-minute
stale threshold *or* substitute a shorter wait by checking with a
pair whose feed has already been stopped for a while, and confirm:
- between 15 and 60 minutes stale, the pair-code text turns amber
  and the tooltip reads "Stale - <timestamp>";
- past 60 minutes stale, the pair-code text turns red with a ⛔
  glyph and the tooltip reads "Disconnected - <timestamp>".
Confirm in all cases that only the pair-code text changes colour --
the rate value itself stays neutral.
