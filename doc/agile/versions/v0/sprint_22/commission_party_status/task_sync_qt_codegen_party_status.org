* Result

Ran the =qt= profile for =party_status= and synced all six generated
files (client model, MDI window, detail dialog, history dialog,
controller, UI) against the current codegen templates. Regenerated
=party_status_protocol.hpp=, =party_status_handler.hpp=,
=party_status_entity.hpp/cpp=, =party_status_repository.cpp=, and
=party_status_service.cpp= in refdata to match.

Review caught two real regressions the mechanical sync introduced,
both fixed at the root cause rather than patched onto generated code:

- =PartyStatusController= silently dropped =ChangeReasonCache= wiring
  because the model was missing =:has_change_reason_cache: true=;
  added the property and regenerated the facet (would have broken
  Create/Amend/Delete for party statuses in the Qt UI).
- The Delete change-reason prompt defaulted to category ="system"=
  (only offers a test-data reason) instead of ="common"=; traced to a
  =cpp_qt_detail_dialog.cpp.mustache= template gap shared by other
  already-merged entities, fixed at the =.org= literate source and
  re-tangled.

A hand-edit of the tangled =.mustache= directly (without updating its
=.org= source) briefly broke the =Template drift= CI check;
corrected by applying the fix to the source and re-tangling.

Filed two follow-up captures for out-of-scope findings:
=fix-client-model-fetch-pagination-no-op= (systemic
=ClientXModel::fetch_*= pagination gap) and
=regen-entities-for-delete-change-reason-category-fix= (regen sweep
for entities synced before the Delete category fix).
