* What

=external/ore/= is a plain vendored directory, not a git submodule
(per =external/ore/manifest.json= and =external/ore/methodology.txt=);
refreshing it from upstream (=OpenSourceRisk/Engine=) is a manual
full-directory copy-and-overwrite with no patch/overlay step. PR
#1423 hand-fixed a reversed =FX/RATE= currency-pair key-order bug
(=USD/GBP=/=USD/CHF= storing =GBP/USD=/=CHF/USD=-shaped values) in
three files confirmed safe to patch locally:
- =external/ore/examples/XvaRisk/Input/market_20160205_eonia_200bp_up.txt=
- =external/ore/examples/XvaRisk/Input/market_20160205_eur6m_200bp_up.txt=
- =external/ore/examples/ORE-Python/Notebooks/Example_7/Input/market.txt=

These edits will be silently lost the next time someone runs the
=external/ore= refresh procedure, since there's no mechanism to
reapply local patches after an overwrite.

* Why

Either document these three hand-patched files in
=external/ore/methodology.txt= so a future refresh knows to reapply
the fix, or track the upstream issue (filed against
=OpenSourceRisk/Engine= covering all instances found, including 4+
more that were investigated and deliberately left unfixed because
their =curveconfig.xml= load-bears the old key) and drop the local
patch once upstream lands it. Flagged during PR #1423 review.
