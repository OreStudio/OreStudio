This is the entry point to the agile information architecture. Work flows as
ideas in the [[id:D4219199-7B17-4A89-B4BC-6019738642DA][product backlog]], promoted into a [[id:699A8D45-B67E-4D3E-9206-24FA17C51ADA][story]] when a [[id:0820B7FD-147C-4832-AC25-C043D38D5B61][sprint]] pulls them in,
broken down into [[id:31C868B9-306E-4282-BA8C-07E647021B34][tasks]] that map one-to-one to pull requests, and grouped into
sprints that compose a [[id:CA4D4BDE-2EAB-4F0A-BC8B-7C0BCB17BC82][version]]. The pages under [[proj:doc/agile][=doc/agile/=]] are:

- [[id:46DFE47F-967B-4C24-95BF-3E21F97CFD20][Agile process]] — overview of the loop: backlog refinement, the three sprint
  phases, version closure; the inventory of the per-concept pages below.
- [[id:4FAC3AE2-04A8-4C35-B72E-78C7A862EED9][Stories]] — what a story is: themes and epics, sizing, ready/done,
  cross-sprint continuations.
- [[id:A7CF5191-6786-408F-A4A6-3A508DCADAC1][Tasks]] — the one-PR unit of execution: sizing limits, discovered tasks,
  ready/done.
- [[id:F5426198-ACAF-41DA-A043-FF15785E4A1D][Sprint planning]] — mission first, choose stories, decompose into tasks.
- [[id:227958A5-0F78-4CC6-A116-B83988592B68][Sprint execution]] — tasks through the lifecycle; heartbeat rule;
  discovered-work triage.
- [[id:7D72A9DA-984F-4130-BFF6-97B66B233907][Sprint closure]] — resolve stories, achievements, release notes,
  retrospective.
- [[id:A064D838-F127-4DD6-BB42-9A7902039AEE][Sprint themes]] — definitions of the six themes and the epic convention used
  to group stories in sprint backlogs.
- [[id:37C10313-E6E9-4D42-B82D-B6758D0A3AD9][Versions]] — every version series of the product, the sprints inside each, and the
  stories and tasks that compose those sprints.
- [[id:C540506E-7129-4CF2-AE3E-6C8BB9C9417F][Milestones]] — the named major release targets each version series works towards;
  owns identity, theme, mission, and definition of done.
- [[id:D4219199-7B17-4A89-B4BC-6019738642DA][Product backlog]] — pre-sprint ideas split into [[id:9A17E8DF-7E94-4F55-8A14-BCB7D9A16D6B][next]] (next-version candidates)
  and [[id:C1BF2C4F-2DB3-49C1-94E9-7D2B3E4F1AB8][deferred]] (longer-term wishlist).

The /contract/ every document follows (frontmatter, sections, state machine) is
described separately under [[id:B69989F6-19E1-430D-AC6D-B2416E4C433F][doc/meta/]]. In particular, [[id:8810E0F9-C5B9-4CFA-A710-774AD6C771DD][lifecycle]] describes TODO
state transitions generically — it is not agile-specific. Agile is the loop
/around/ those states.
