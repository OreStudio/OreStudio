#+title: Role-Based Access Control
#+description: The RBAC model — roles compose permissions, users are assigned roles — as applied in ORE Studio's IAM subsystem.

/Role-Based Access Control (RBAC)/ restricts system access to
authorised users based on their job roles, rather than individual
user permissions. By grouping permissions into roles (e.g. =admin=,
=user=), the project simplifies access management, enforces least
privilege, and lowers the blast radius of any one compromised
account. RBAC is the model that backs [[id:8AEB87D6-0CE2-1434-E71B-60BEAE20ADF0][Identity and Access
Management]] in ORE Studio; the concrete role/permission entities and
their assignments live in [[id:D00BEA0D-E501-C534-A013-E6F40C1A6097][ores.iam.core]]. Return to [[id:7474935C-95D1-3034-B833-83DCD9FB3A09][Knowledge]].

* Key Components of RBAC

- *Roles*: collections of permissions defining job functions
  (/e.g./, "Nurse", "Manager").
- *Permissions*: specific rights to view, create, or modify data.
- *Users*: individuals assigned one or more roles.

* Core Principles

- *Role-Based Assignment*: users are assigned roles, and
  permissions are attached to those roles, not the user directly.
- *Least Privilege*: users receive only the minimum permissions
  necessary for their role.
- *Separation of Duties (SoD)*: As described in [[https://www.ibm.com/br-pt/think/topics/rbac][IBM's research]], SoD
  prevents conflicts of interest by requiring multiple people to
  complete high-risk tasks.

* Benefits of RBAC

- *Simplified Management*: reduces the overhead of managing
  permissions for thousands of users.
- *Increased Security*: according to the [[https://www.cloudflare.com/en-gb/learning/access-management/role-based-access-control-rbac/][Cloudflare write-up]], it
  limits unauthorised access and reduces the risk of data leaks.
- *Compliance*: facilitates adherence to data-protection
  regulations.
