#+title: Market Simulator option to show data in pips
#+description: Add a configuration toggle in the Market Simulator to display prices and ranges in pips rather than absolute rate.
#+type: capture
#+level: cross

This page is a [[id:671F18E4-E09C-4B3B-BD24-D33DF8AE38A6][capture]] in the *deferred* bucket of the product backlog — a pre-sprint idea, not yet pulled into a sprint as a story.

* What

Add a configuration toggle to the Market Simulator that switches the
sample-paths display (and any other rate readouts) between absolute rate
and *pips*. When enabled, the sample-paths Y-axis, the realised-range
status note, and price-derived figures would be expressed as pip
movement from a reference (typically the initial price), using the
pair's pip size (1e-4 for most pairs, 1e-2 for JPY crosses). The toggle
could live on the Price-behaviour tab or as a view option on the
=SamplePricePathsChart= toolbar
(=projects/ores.qt/synthetic/src/SamplePricePathsChart.cpp=), with the
Y-axis label format and range readout switching accordingly.

* Why

FX practitioners think in pips, not absolute rate. Expressing the
simulated movement in pips makes the volatility profiles (Calm / Normal
/ Volatile) immediately intuitive and comparable to real desk
conventions, complementing the recently-added realised-range readout
which is currently shown in absolute rate. It also sidesteps the
small-number Y-axis where 0.025% vs 0.4% volatility is hard to read in
the fourth decimal.
