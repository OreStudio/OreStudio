* Summary

Currency, in ORE Studio's domain model, rests on a conceptual
foundation before it becomes a system of ISO-coded, trading-desk
reference data: [[id:C52FF609-FCFD-4F7D-8F5F-121212132ADB][money]] as a social convention, narrowed to a [[id:C17C8215-DB6C-4E1A-BF88-63ABB7E145E0][kind]]
and a standardised or non-standardised code, and narrowed further
still to [[id:A281A64C-5A52-4747-BBDD-3CECB7A72F3C][cash]] at the liquid end of the spectrum. A currency also has
its own [[id:6AC99797-D97B-463C-9351-4946DC80D91C][lifecycle]], independent of any position denominated in it.
Once a trading system is layered on top, a single trade or book does
not carry just one "currency" -- the same position is described in
several different currencies at once, each serving a distinct
purpose: a [[id:E4FC332F-C404-4408-8E10-586FAF4ECCFC][functional]] currency for the ledger, a [[id:ACB7ED8A-0E01-47D5-8A3B-FE59FDF7C000][reporting]]
currency for display, a [[id:4ADDDA46-21CE-4C94-9F22-F66805B13891][transaction currency / denominated currency]]
for a deal's own cash flows, an [[id:A7338353-8D2F-4784-92C0-B6E338C0D9C4][aggregation]] currency for cross-currency risk
comparison, and narrower roles still for [[id:2F7AD024-2CDF-4B31-9845-0879179B0D1A][settlement]], [[id:1E27EF8E-FBA2-4D3F-B4E5-5B1C81F07522][premium]], and
which currencies a [[id:C5561A84-D060-4AB4-AB40-4ABC08875040][book]] is even permitted to hold. The mechanisms that
convert and reconcile between these roles -- [[id:CA299D08-AC97-4A37-B3F2-A5927F379326][sweeps]],
[[id:0CAD6D4D-604A-4FB9-A823-5231E759D760][FX spot revaluation]], and the [[id:10E36403-847A-4D0A-B532-E7DA0EE0B670][closing rates screen]] that reviews the
rate they use -- are what keep this multiplicity of currencies
tractable rather than a source of silent inconsistency.

* Detail

** The conceptual foundation: money, currency, cash

Before currency becomes trading-system reference data, it is a
narrowing chain of three related but distinct concepts. [[id:C52FF609-FCFD-4F7D-8F5F-121212132ADB][Money]] is
the broadest: any generally-accepted, verifiable record of value,
defined by the four functions it performs rather than by its physical
form. [[id:C17C8215-DB6C-4E1A-BF88-63ABB7E145E0][Kinds of Money]] surveys the historical forms that convention has
taken -- commodity, fiat, bank money, and the modern, deliberately
ambiguous case of cryptocurrency. Currency itself narrows money down
to a system of money in common use within an area of circulation,
standardised by [[id:00F4B89E-E216-49BB-8BEC-7BCEE7BDFBBD][ISO 4217]] or left outside that standard entirely, and
possessing its own [[id:6AC99797-D97B-463C-9351-4946DC80D91C][lifecycle]] of creation, circulation, and eventual
withdrawal. [[id:A281A64C-5A52-4747-BBDD-3CECB7A72F3C][Cash]] narrows the chain a final step, to the most liquid
instances of a currency, and brings with it the broader vocabulary of
[[id:AC6DAC84-8BFA-4E3A-BEF9-D747D920E7AB][assets]], [[id:0F727383-5B40-4366-91EF-36319F215997][liquidity]], and [[id:13341B12-0393-4ECF-B392-CD68A4C0CDFB][cash flow]] that cash sits within.

** Currency as a role: what a book or trade actually carries

A single position is never described by only one currency once a
booking, ledger, and risk system is layered on top of the
conceptual foundation above. [[id:E4FC332F-C404-4408-8E10-586FAF4ECCFC][Functional currency]] is the ledger's own
accounting currency, dictated by a book's legal jurisdiction rather
than freely chosen. [[id:ACB7ED8A-0E01-47D5-8A3B-FE59FDF7C000][Presentation currency]] is a purely presentational,
live-spot conversion with no risk consequence of its own, best kept
equal to aggregation currency for consistency. [[id:4ADDDA46-21CE-4C94-9F22-F66805B13891][Transaction currency]]
and *denominated currency* describe the currency in which a
deal's own cash flows or results are properly expressed before any
cross-currency comparison is attempted. [[id:A7338353-8D2F-4784-92C0-B6E338C0D9C4][Aggregation currency]] is what a
risk engine converts every amount into so that deals with different
denominated currencies become comparable -- a conversion that, unlike
presentation currency's, generates real risk rather than merely
displaying a number differently. Two narrower roles round out the
set: [[id:2F7AD024-2CDF-4B31-9845-0879179B0D1A][settlement currency]] governs a trade's settlement-day calendar
and cash-settlement payment, and [[id:1E27EF8E-FBA2-4D3F-B4E5-5B1C81F07522][premium currency]] governs the currency
an option's premium is paid in -- itself the denominated currency
an option resolves to, since no single transactional currency
otherwise applies. [[id:C5561A84-D060-4AB4-AB40-4ABC08875040][Book currencies]] sits apart from the rest of this list: rather
than describing how a position is expressed, it constrains which
currencies a book may hold an instrument in at all.

** Conversion and reconciliation mechanics

A system carrying this many simultaneous currency roles needs
mechanisms that keep them converted and reconciled against one
another rather than silently drifting apart. [[id:CA299D08-AC97-4A37-B3F2-A5927F379326][Sweep]] documents the
two distinct sweep mechanisms: a periodic,
front/middle-office spot sweep crystallising non-presentation-currency
profit, and a daily, automated, accounting-layer ledger sweep
translating transactional balances into functional currency. [[id:0CAD6D4D-604A-4FB9-A823-5231E759D760][FX
Spot Revaluation Types]] breaks that ledger-sweep translation down
further into the three accounting treatments settled and unsettled FX
require -- Balance Sheet, Position Account, and Forward Position
reval. [[id:10E36403-847A-4D0A-B532-E7DA0EE0B670][Closing Rates Screen]] is where the automated End-of-Day rate
these mechanisms depend on meets human review, letting a stale or
misquoted rate be caught and, if needed, overridden before it
propagates into every downstream EOD figure. Beyond the currency
cluster itself, [[id:DC08D216-348D-4511-A42D-4016EBBF38F7][CRM risk: recentering and artefacts]] and [[id:D215AB01-8BE4-4E2B-930C-17AC80491C9D][Book and
the ledger]] work through the fuller consequences of aggregation
currency and functional currency respectively, at the level of an
entire cross-rates matrix and an entire accounting hierarchy.
