#+title: Migrate remaining refdata entities to generated event-mapping registrar
#+description: Resolve nats-eventing drift for 10 refdata entities so their live-eventing wiring can move from hand-wired application.cpp to the generated per-entity event registrar.

This page is a [[id:671F18E4-E09C-4B3B-BD24-D33DF8AE38A6][capture]] in the *inbox* bucket of the product backlog — a pre-sprint idea, not yet pulled into a sprint as a story.

* What

Regenerate =ores.cpp.nats-eventing= for =business_unit=, =counterparty=,
=counterparty_contact_information=, =counterparty_identifier=,
=currency_market_tier=, =monetary_nature=, =party=,
=party_contact_information=, =party_identifier=, and =portfolio= —
each currently has stale =changed_event.hpp= output where the id field
is plain =ids= instead of the current template's
={{entity_singular_short}}_ids=. Check every existing consumer of the
old field name for each entity (Qt controllers, handlers, anything else
referencing =.ids= on that entity's changed_event) before regenerating,
since the field rename is a breaking change to any such consumer. Once
each entity's =nats-eventing= output is current, add its
=register_<entity>_event_mapping()= call to
=ores::refdata::service::messaging::event_registrar::register_event_mappings=
and remove its hand-wired block from =application.cpp=.

* Why

Filed while wiring the generated per-entity NATS event-mapping registrar
(story 9763FBBF-7912-4EA2-A544-67E90EA27882): only 6 of 16 currently
hand-wired =application.cpp= entities (=purpose_type=, =book=,
=business_day_convention_type=, =country=, =currency=, =party_type=) had
current =nats-eventing= output and could be migrated immediately. The
other 10 were left hand-wired rather than risk a field-rename regression
by regenerating their changed_event structs as a side effect of an
unrelated registrar migration.
