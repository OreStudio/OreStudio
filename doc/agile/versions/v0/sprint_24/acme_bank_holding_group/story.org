* Goal

Barclays Plc has been our stand-in test/demo legal entity because it
has a "realistic" corporate shape, but it is not fit for purpose: it
has no employees, so we cannot exercise features that depend on
staff/role identity — e.g. verifying that logging in as Middle
Office, or as a Trader, resolves the correct permissions and data
visibility.

Introduce *Acme Bank* — a synthetic holding-group legal entity,
structured after Barclays' real corporate shape (holding company →
Retail Banking, Investment Bank, and other divisions, across
multiple global business centres) — and populate it fully across
every entity ORE Studio already supports: parties, business units,
portfolios, books, and staff accounts. Not every business
unit/portfolio/book combination needs to exist, but every division
that trades derivatives must have a complete chain down to book
level. Staff accounts should exist for the roles needed to exercise
RBAC (trader, middle office, etc.), with names generated to match
the locale of their business centre (e.g. Korean names for the Seoul
desk) and a profile picture per staff member.

Acme Bank becomes the system's reference "fully populated" entity —
extended over time as new entities and features are added, so it
stays comprehensive rather than needing to be rebuilt from scratch
each time.

* Acceptance

- A holding-group party hierarchy exists for Acme Bank, modelled on
  Barclays' real corporate structure: a top-level holding company
  with subsidiaries for at least Retail Banking and Investment
  Banking, spread across multiple business centres/locations.
- Every subsidiary/division that trades derivatives has a full chain
  of business units, portfolios, and books underneath it — divisions
  that don't trade derivatives may stop at the party/business-unit
  level.
- Staff accounts exist for the roles needed to exercise RBAC (at
  minimum: trader, middle office), attached to the business
  units/desks they belong to, with correct role/permission
  assignments.
- Staff names are generated to match the locale of their business
  centre (e.g. Korean names for a Seoul desk, not generic Western
  names everywhere).
- Every generated staff account has an associated profile picture.
- The generation is repeatable/scriptable (generator, not a one-off
  manual data entry exercise) so the entity can be regenerated and
  extended as new entities/features land.
- Data is seeded through the same provisioning path used for other
  tenants (see how Barclays Plc is currently provisioned), not via
  hand-written SQL.

* Decisions

- *Acme owns everything.* Existing orphaned business-unit/portfolio/
  book/report example fixtures get re-parented under Acme rather than
  staying dangling test data with no real owning party.
- *Integrate, don't invent a world.* Unlike =slovaris=, Acme is not a
  self-contained fictional universe: its own legal entities carry
  fake-but-checksum-valid LEIs (GLEIF's reserved =9695= pre-LOU test
  prefix), but its counterparties are the real GLEIF-imported set —
  the same one Barclays trades against.
- *Business-unit/portfolio/book population is deferred* until
  prime_origin's unified DQ dataset-publish refactor lands, to avoid
  building bespoke seeding that would be thrown away.
- *Three branches* (London, New York, Hong Kong) — enough for
  follow-the-sun trading without needing "the biggest bank."
- *Account pictures*: generalise the existing =ores_assets_images_tbl=
  (currently SVG-only) to a binary-agnostic asset table, rather than
  add a parallel face-images table. Implemented as =mime_type= +
  base64-encoded =data= (text, not =bytea= — no existing pqxx/mapper
  precedent for binary columns in this codebase); the DQ
  artefact-staging table stays untouched SVG-only text, since it's
  inherently a flag/GLEIF-style import pipeline, not a generic image
  store.
- *Account =image_id= is a plain UUID with a nil sentinel, not
  =std::optional=.* A second =std::optional<boost::uuids::uuid>= field
  on =ores::iam::domain::account= triggers a real reflect-cpp
  field-count-detection bug (segfault/corrupted JSON on multi-element
  vectors) specific to that struct; =boost::uuids::nil_uuid()= as
  "unset" is an existing codebase convention and sidesteps it.

* Out of scope

- Retiring or removing Barclays Plc — Acme Bank supplements it as the
  richer/fully-populated entity; Barclays Plc may still be useful for
  simpler/lighter-weight test scenarios.
- Modelling every possible business unit/portfolio/book combination
  across non-derivative-trading divisions — only derivative-trading
  divisions need the full chain (see Acceptance).
- Actual trade/position data — this story is about the corporate,
  organisational, and staff structure; trade population is a
  follow-on concern.
