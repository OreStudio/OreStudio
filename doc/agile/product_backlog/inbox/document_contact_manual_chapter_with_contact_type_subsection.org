#+title: Document Contact/Contact Information with contact_type as a subsection
#+description: Write the Contact/Contact Information manual chapter with contact_type folded in as its valid-values subsection, per the aux-type documentation convention.

This page is a [[id:671F18E4-E09C-4B3B-BD24-D33DF8AE38A6][capture]] in the *inbox* bucket of the product backlog — a pre-sprint idea, not yet pulled into a sprint as a story.

* What

Write the user-manual chapter for Contact / Contact Information (party
and counterparty contact records), following the thesis structure used
by other entity chapters. Fold =contact_type= in as that chapter's
"valid values" subsection — code/name/description/display_order,
listed and screenshotted from Reference Data > Organisation Codes >
Contact Types — rather than giving =contact_type= a standalone
chapter of its own.

* Why

Discovered while commissioning [[id:E1F9C83E-645D-4CFC-B1C9-656651C55BCD][contact_type]]:
=contact_type= is an auxiliary/lookup type, and the
[[id:897A7156-504D-4581-AD4C-026D643C0838][Domain entity evaluation checklist]] is explicit that aux types don't
get their own manual chapter — they get a subsection in the manual
chapter of the primary entity that consumes them. That consuming
entity is Contact / Contact Information, which has no manual chapter
of its own yet. Writing a standalone =contact_type= chapter would have
documented the lookup without the thing it looks up into.
