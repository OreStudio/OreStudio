* What

Paper abstract: "Machine-generated policies can be produced faster
than humans can review them, while testing observes only sampled
behavior. We present a Lean 4 reference architecture that separates
proposal from modeled authority at a pre-trade risk boundary. A
candidate becomes active only after contract-relative qualification
and promotion bind acceptance to its exact bytes. The gateway derives
a request from root-owned parameters, trusted snapshots, and live
reservations; evaluates the decoded promoted artifact; admits only
requests in the root-owned finite evidence domain; and reserves risk
before modeled dispatch. For a sequential, buy-only, one-account,
one-instrument, two-slot machine from exact zero genesis, we prove
that every valid finite trace preserves position and limit-price-basis
acquisition-commitment caps, with commitment also bounded by cash
headroom. For each archived dispatch, the theorem furnishes
artifact-causality, root-agreement, and exact-origin witnesses; a
separate theorem rejects off-domain requests fail-closed. These
model-internal results assume named signature, snapshot, kernel,
toolchain, operating-system, and hardware trust boundaries. They
establish neither concurrency nor linearizability, Lean-Rust
refinement, concrete cryptographic security, external-fact truth,
liveness, nor economic or regulatory correctness. Rust supplies
regression evidence through pinned tests and Lean-emitted fixtures.
The reproduction gate checks pinned-source replay, a frozen axiom
inventory, generated theorem signatures and claim tables, and
normalized-PDF agreement across two same-environment builds;
ledger-prose fidelity remains human-reviewed."

In plain terms: a machine-checked (Lean 4 theorem-proven) risk gate
sitting between an automated policy/strategy proposer and actual
trade dispatch. Policies only go live once qualified and "promoted"
byte-for-byte; the gateway then only ever evaluates that exact
promoted artifact against root-owned parameters/snapshots/live
reservations, proving (for a constrained sequential single-account/
instrument model) that position and commitment caps — including
cash headroom — can never be breached, and that off-domain requests
are rejected fail-closed. Explicitly scoped: no concurrency/
linearizability claims, no Lean-to-Rust refinement proof, no
cryptographic-security or liveness claims, no economic/regulatory
correctness claims — it's a narrow, honestly-scoped formal safety
net around one specific risk boundary, not a general trading-system
correctness proof.
