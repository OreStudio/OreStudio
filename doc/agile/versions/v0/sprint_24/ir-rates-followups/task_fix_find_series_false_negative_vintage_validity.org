* Goal

Fix a false-negative in the Market Simulator's vintage-validity
check: every FX feed shows a red (invalid) badge even for themes
with genuinely correct, matching vintage data. Verified directly in
Postgres for Barclays Plc's 2016 ORE Samples and Uniform Volatility
Demo themes -- =ores_marketdata_market_observations_tbl= has all 13
=fed.h10.2016-02-05= observations, correctly scoped to the party, and
=ores_marketdata_market_series_tbl= has matching
=(series_type, metric, qualifier)= rows for every pair (e.g.
=FX/RATE/EUR/USD=). Despite this, tracing
=ores.synthetic.service.0.log= during a reload's vintage check (37
feeds) shows =market_observations.list= is never called even once --
meaning
=feed_controller::vintage_data_available()='s call to
=market_data_client::find_series()=
(=projects/ores.marketdata/client/src/market_data_client.cpp:111=)
is failing to match a series that demonstrably exists in its own
NATS response, for every single feed, and the code returns "no
vintage data" before ever reaching the observations lookup.

Root-cause the mismatch in =find_series()= (or the
=get_market_series_request=/=response= round trip it depends on) and
fix it. This is independent of the "2026 Realistic has no published
vintage data at all" gap (tracked separately) -- this bug makes
themes that DO have correct data show as invalid too.
