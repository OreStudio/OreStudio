note is a single focused concept; start here and follow the links.

* Summary

A *currency pair* is the fundamental unit of FX trading: it defines the
exchange relationship between two currencies and carries all the
conventions needed to quote, settle, and value a transaction. Almost
every FX concept — spot rates, forward curves, vol surfaces, fixings — is
anchored to a pair rather than to a currency in isolation. This cluster
covers what a currency pair *is* as a data entity: how it is named, which
currency is base, deliverability, quoting/pip conventions, and
classification. It is a companion to the
[[id:48F6FD8D-56C7-4FF7-A369-CD13D08A6057][cross-rates matrix]] cluster, which covers spot-rate derivation and
graph structure rather than the pair entity itself.
