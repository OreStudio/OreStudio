#+title: Add FX market-convention facet (base/quote order, pip precision)
#+description: No reusable facet exists for FX market conventions (canonical base/quote ordering per pair, pip/decimal precision for display) -- rates are formatted as plain doubles everywhere they're shown.
#+type: capture
#+level: cross
#+filetags: :refdata:fx:marketdata:ui:
#+created: 2026-07-14
#+updated: 2026-07-14

This page is a [[id:671F18E4-E09C-4B3B-BD24-D33DF8AE38A6][capture]] in the *inbox* bucket of the product backlog — a pre-sprint idea, not yet pulled into a sprint as a story.

* What

Add a reusable FX market-convention lookup (likely a small refdata
entity or static table keyed by currency pair) capturing, per pair:
the canonical base/quote ordering the market actually quotes in
(e.g. EUR/USD, not USD/EUR; GBP/USD not USD/GBP) and the standard
display precision (pip size / decimal places -- e.g. most pairs to
4-5 decimals, JPY crosses to 2-3). Today `crm_rate_item::rate` and
every other rate display in the codebase is a bare `double`, always
formatted the same way regardless of pair (see
`CrmCrossRatesMatrixMdiWindow::reload()`'s
`QString::number(item.rate, 'f', 5)` for one instance) and there is
no ordering convention applied anywhere -- driver/derived pairs are
stored and displayed in whatever base/quote order they were
generated or configured in.

* Why

Surfaced while building the CRM cross-rates matrix UI panel (see
[[id:C685A8BE-0DF9-4D23-8AAB-E9DF9069E482][the task]]), whose reference mockup
(`doc/analysis/gemini_cross_rates_matrix.png`) shows convention-aware
formatting (JPY pairs to fewer decimals, consistent base/quote
ordering) that the codebase has no facility to produce. This isn't
specific to the matrix panel -- any place a rate or currency pair is
displayed (feed bindings, market data feeds, trading tickets) would
benefit from a single source of truth for market convention instead
of ad hoc fixed-precision formatting per call site.
