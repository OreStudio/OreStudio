* Goal

Build the foundational, persisted *synthetic market data configuration* and
make the synthetic service generate from it — which fixes the system-tenant
defect and gives every observation provenance.

Model (agreed 2026-06-28):
- Lives in =ores.synthetic= (it is a *synthetic* config, not a generic market
  feed config).
- Scoped to *tenant + party*: each party has its own config(s) and its own
  synthetic data.
- A *top-level container* holds *typed sub-configs* — one per market-data type
  (FX spot now; vol surface, interest-rate curves, … later) so new types are
  added as child rows without touching the container.
- Each sub-config has a stable *source name* (e.g. =synthetic.gmm123=).
- The synthetic service loads *enabled* configs (replacing the boot-time
  hardcoded EUR/USD-as-system bootstrap) and, per sub-config: resolves/creates
  the series under the config's *tenant*, generates, and stamps every
  observation with that *tenant* and the *source name*.
- *Provenance via source name*: market_series/market_observation stay
  tenant-scoped (no new party column); the source name ties data back to the
  party's config. The service account remains the trusted writer (writes land
  because the tenant only needs to exist — see investigation below).

Out of scope here (separate tasks): the synthetic config CRUD UI, and the
generic (source-agnostic) market data control panel.

* Acceptance

- The target tenant is supplied as feed configuration (start request /
  persisted named config), not the synthetic service's own tenant.
- The EUR/USD series and its observations are created under that business
  tenant; the series bootstrap happens at feed-start, not service startup.
- A business-tenant login sees the series/observations in Market Series and the
  chart; the system tenant is no longer used for synthetic market data.
- Writes are authorised as the target tenant (delegation or equivalent), not a
  cross-tenant system write.
- Decision recorded: how the synthetic service obtains tenant-scoped authority
  for the write.

* Plan

** Investigation (2026-06-28)

Tenant model constraints found in the marketdata stack:

- Writes are NOT session-tenant-enforced: the observations insert trigger only
  validates the tenant exists (=marketdata_observations_create.sql:89=,
  =ores_iam_validate_tenant_fn=), and =market_observation_mapper= writes the
  *entity's* =tenant_id=. So a privileged caller can write a row stamped with
  any existing tenant.
- Reads/writes derive the tenant from the caller's JWT:
  =make_request_context= sets ctx tenant from the synthetic service account
  (= system). =series.list= is scoped to ctx tenant, so synthetic (system)
  cannot list a business tenant's series to check existence before create.
- True delegation is not feasible for an autonomous feed: there is no end-user
  JWT to forward, and synthetic cannot mint a token for an arbitrary tenant.

** Options

- (A) Privileged explicit-tenant writes: feed config carries the target
  tenant; stamp series + observations with it (writes land since only
  existence is validated). Resolve series existence via an explicit-tenant
  lookup — extend =series.list=/=get= to accept a target tenant for privileged
  service callers, or create-and-handle-exists. Synthetic stays a system
  service. Pragmatic; smallest change that satisfies the goal.
- (B) Market data is shared/global: treat market data as system/shared
  reference data readable by all tenants (FX rates are not tenant-private);
  fix the read path so business logins see it. Avoids cross-tenant writes —
  but contradicts "do not generate under the system tenant".
- (C) Per-tenant synthetic identity: synthetic authenticates per target tenant
  (real delegation), writing natively under that tenant. Cleanest security,
  largest change (per-tenant service credentials / tenant-scoped tokens).

Recommendation: (A) for the PoC, noting (C) as the long-term hardening.
Awaiting decision before implementing.
