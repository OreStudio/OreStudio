#+description: How the project's agile loop runs — backlog refinement, opening a sprint, sizing tasks, closing a sprint, and closing a version.
#+type: knowledge
#+level: cross
#+filetags: :agile:process:sprint:backlog:knowledge:

This doc describes how the agile loop runs in practice — distinct from
the generic [[id:8810E0F9-C5B9-4CFA-A710-774AD6C771DD][lifecycle]] (which covers TODO state transitions across
every stateful document type). Lifecycle is the state machine on each
document; this is the rhythm /around/ those documents: how ideas move
from the [[id:D4219199-7B17-4A89-B4BC-6019738642DA][product backlog]] into a [[id:0820B7FD-147C-4832-AC25-C043D38D5B61][sprint]] as [[id:699A8D45-B67E-4D3E-9206-24FA17C51ADA][stories]], how those stories
decompose into [[id:31C868B9-306E-4282-BA8C-07E647021B34][tasks]], and how a sprint opens, runs, and closes.

* The loop

A single turn of the loop:

1. /Refine/ the [[id:D4219199-7B17-4A89-B4BC-6019738642DA][product backlog]] — keep the [[id:9A17E8DF-7E94-4F55-8A14-BCB7D9A16D6B][next]] bucket honest as
   candidates change shape, and let new ideas land in [[id:C1BF2C4F-2DB3-49C1-94E9-7D2B3E4F1AB8][deferred]].
2. /Plan/ a new sprint — define the [[id:198DA730-D9FB-4BDE-B1C0-B930CD019917][sprint mission]] first (it
   orientates which stories to pull), then choose a small set of
   [[id:671F18E4-E09C-4B3B-BD24-D33DF8AE38A6][captures]] from next that fit the mission and promote each into a
   [[id:699A8D45-B67E-4D3E-9206-24FA17C51ADA][story]]. Break stories into one-PR-sized tasks.
3. /Execute/ — agents pick up =BACKLOG= tasks, transition to =STARTED=,
   open a PR, land it, mark =DONE=. The heartbeat rule keeps Status
   fresh.
4. /Close/ the sprint — fill the retrospective, generate release
   notes, cut a release if applicable, transition every story to
   =DONE= / =ABANDONED= / carried, the sprint itself to =DONE=.
5. /Next/ sprint opens. New captures discovered during execution have
   already landed in the backlog and surface in the next refinement.

A version is the outer loop: a coherent group of sprints with a
shared mission. It opens when its first sprint opens, and closes when
the version's [[id:8B29A8D8-E004-4E87-B81D-ABEAF68F2B98][Definition of done]] is met.

* Detail

** Backlog refinement

*Owned by* [[id:8F13BA75-EDAA-46CC-9BE2-E356D60BF4F4][System 3]] (sprint coherence drives whether next is honest).
Captures are filed by [[id:BF4F183F-BB33-44F2-A53F-D9BD602E9BE8][System 2]] when discovered during execution.
Identity-fit calls are referred to [[id:6DF00A64-7CC3-40A7-950B-55D2B05A31C7][System 5]]; next↔deferred moves for the
next version are driven by [[id:6CA8B181-BD81-4EF4-AF27-64F5491D62F0][System 4]].

Refinement is the cheap, ongoing work that keeps the [[id:9A17E8DF-7E94-4F55-8A14-BCB7D9A16D6B][next]] bucket
usable. It is /not/ a meeting — it is small touches whenever a
capture is touched:

- New ideas land via =compass add capture=. Pick the
  bucket using the rules in [[id:D4219199-7B17-4A89-B4BC-6019738642DA][product backlog]].
- A capture's body may be tightened (description, tags) as a sprint
  approaches and it becomes a serious candidate.
- A capture moves =git mv= between buckets when its horizon changes
  (e.g. a "deferred" idea becomes "next" because its blocking dependency
  landed; a "next" idea drops to "deferred" because the next version's
  focus shifted).
- Captures that are no longer interesting are *deleted*, not
  archived. The product backlog is not an artefact museum.

A capture file carries no Status table — there is nothing to update
during refinement except its body and bucket.

** Sprint phases

A sprint moves through three phases. Each phase is a distinct mode of
work; the phase boundaries are sharp.

*** Sprint planning
:PROPERTIES:
:ID: F5426198-ACAF-41DA-A043-FF15785E4A1D
:END:

*Owned by* [[id:8F13BA75-EDAA-46CC-9BE2-E356D60BF4F4][System 3]]. [[id:6DF00A64-7CC3-40A7-950B-55D2B05A31C7][System 5]] vets each candidate against the
[[id:2F71292F-CDB0-4E2E-B50F-4F02E10597C4][product vision]]; [[id:6CA8B181-BD81-4EF4-AF27-64F5491D62F0][System 4]] sanity-checks the sprint mission
against the version mission; [[id:BF4F183F-BB33-44F2-A53F-D9BD602E9BE8][System 2]] decomposes each promoted
story into tasks.

A sprint exists when its folder exists. To open sprint N+1:

1. *Define the sprint mission.* One sentence: what this sprint exists
   to achieve. The mission is the /filter/ that decides whether
   borderline captures make it in. Write it before looking at the
   backlog so the choice of stories is led by the mission, not the
   other way round. See [[id:198DA730-D9FB-4BDE-B1C0-B930CD019917][sprint mission]].
2. *Choose stories.* Look at the [[id:9A17E8DF-7E94-4F55-8A14-BCB7D9A16D6B][next]] bucket; pick captures whose
   combined size fits one sprint's budget (see /Sizing/ below) /and/
   whose theme matches the mission. Captures that don't fit the
   mission stay in the bucket.
3. *Create the sprint folder.* Use compass: =compass add sprint
   --parent-dir doc/agile/versions/<version>=. Slug is
   =sprint_NN=.
4. *Promote each chosen capture.* For each:
   - =git mv= the capture file from its bucket into
     =doc/agile/versions/<version>/<sprint_NN>/<slug>/story.org=.
   - Flip =#+type:= from =capture= to =story=. The =:ID:= is preserved.
   - Apply the v2 story shape: blurb, =* Goal=, =* Status= (table,
     state =BACKLOG=), =* Acceptance=, =* Tasks= (empty), =* Decisions=,
     =* Out of scope=.
   - Re-run =regenerate_backlog_indexes.py=; the entry disappears from
     the bucket index automatically.
5. *Decompose stories into tasks.* Each task is one PR; see /Sizing/.
   Generate task files with =compass add task
   --parent-dir <story-dir>=. Order them in the story's =* Tasks=
   table by execution order. Tasks start =BACKLOG=.
6. *Regenerate the version's sprint table* on the version page.

*** Sprint execution
:PROPERTIES:
:ID: 227958A5-0F78-4CC6-A116-B83988592B68
:END:

*Owned by* [[id:DBFD530B-4B56-48D1-9F03-3A0D0DA16552][System 1]] (does the work). [[id:BF4F183F-BB33-44F2-A53F-D9BD602E9BE8][System 2]] reviews S1 output and
triages discovered tasks; [[id:ED29C99B-1507-49C9-9937-D296FFFE428E][System 3*]] audits the heartbeat
invariant and id-link integrity.

Agents pick up =BACKLOG= tasks and run them through the [[id:8810E0F9-C5B9-4CFA-A710-774AD6C771DD][lifecycle]]
state machine: =BACKLOG → STARTED → (maybe BLOCKED → STARTED) → DONE=.
Each task is one PR.

The /heartbeat rule/ applies: every time an orchestrator or agent
acts on a task or story, it updates the document's Status rows
(=Now / Waiting on / Next / Last touched=) and =#+updated= before
yielding control. Audit flags =STARTED= or =BLOCKED= documents whose
=#+updated= has gone stale.

Discovered tasks during execution land in =DISCOVERED= state at the
sprint's =inbox/= if they don't fit any existing story, or under the
current story if they do. They must leave =DISCOVERED= within one
orchestrator session (see [[id:8810E0F9-C5B9-4CFA-A710-774AD6C771DD][lifecycle]]).

*** Sprint closure
:PROPERTIES:
:ID: 7D72A9DA-984F-4130-BFF6-97B66B233907
:END:

*Owned by* [[id:8F13BA75-EDAA-46CC-9BE2-E356D60BF4F4][System 3]] (drives the sequence). [[id:BF4F183F-BB33-44F2-A53F-D9BD602E9BE8][System 2]] resolves each
story (=DONE= / =ABANDONED= / carried); [[id:6CA8B181-BD81-4EF4-AF27-64F5491D62F0][System 4]] weighs in on whether
to cut a release.

Closure is more than transitioning to =DONE=. The sequence is:

1. *Resolve every story.* Every story in the sprint is =DONE=,
   =ABANDONED=, or has been explicitly carried to a successor (see
   /Cross-sprint continuations/).
2. *Write the Achievements.* Add a =** Achievements= sub-heading
   inside =* Status=, after the Status table. Write 3–7 bullet points
   capturing what the sprint actually delivered — specific outcomes,
   not story titles. Clear the =Now= field to =Nothing.= at the same
   time. Achievements are only written at sprint close; the heading
   does not appear in in-flight sprints.
3. *Generate the release notes* if a release is being cut. The notes
   are produced from the closed tasks' =* Result= sections and the
   PRs that landed.
4. *Cut the release* (tag, build, publish artefacts) when the version
   warrants it. Not every sprint produces a release; many sprints
   land mid-version. See [[id:B69989F6-19E1-430D-AC6D-B2416E4C433F][Compass]] for what the release tag means in
   terms of CMake major/minor.
5. *Run the post-mortem.* Fill the sprint's =* Retrospective= section
   — what went well, what hurt, what changed. Past sprints under
   =doc/agile/versions/v0/sprint_*/= are the template.
6. *Transition the sprint* itself to =DONE= and update =Last touched=
   on the Status row.

Sprint closure ends when all five steps are complete; the next
sprint's planning phase can then begin.

** Closing a version

A version closes when its [[id:8B29A8D8-E004-4E87-B81D-ABEAF68F2B98][Definition of done]] is met. The version
file's Status moves =STARTED → RELEASED=, =:CLOSED:= is stamped, and
the Sprints table on the version page is the historical record.

** Cross-sprint continuations

A story belongs to exactly /one/ sprint. When work doesn't fit:

1. Close the story in sprint N as =DONE= reflecting what landed.
2. Tasks that didn't make it are recorded in the predecessor's
   =* Out of scope= or moved to the successor's =BACKLOG=.
3. Create a /successor/ story in sprint N+1 with:
   - A fresh =:ID:=; slug suffixed =_continued=, =_phase_2=, or similar.
   - =#+predecessor:= frontmatter pointing at the prior story.
   - A =Continued from:= line under the parent-sprint link.
4. Update the predecessor:
   - =#+successor:= frontmatter pointing forward.
   - =Continued in:= note in =* Decisions=.

Audit verifies the bidirectional link.

The same pattern applies across version boundaries.

** Sizing

Hard rule: *one task = one PR*. Soft limits per task:

- ≤ ~10 files changed.
- ≤ ~400 LOC delta.
- One coherent concept — a reviewer can hold the change in their head.

Tasks exceeding any of these split into multiple tasks, or — if the
work is genuinely one outcome and just large — promote to a story
whose tasks are the natural decomposition.

Stories are sized by their tasks: a story with more than ~10 tasks is
usually two stories.

Sprints are time-boxed at roughly one mission's worth of work. The
specific budget is empirical (look at past sprints' task counts and
elapsed days under =doc/agile/versions/v0/=) rather than a fixed
hour-count.

** "Ready" and "Done" at each level

A document is /ready/ to leave =BACKLOG= when:

- *Task* — Goal and Acceptance are filled; parent story exists.
- *Story* — Goal, Acceptance, and at least the first few Tasks are
  filled; parent sprint exists.
- *Sprint* — Mission is filled; at least one story is BACKLOG-ready.

A document is /done/ when:

- *Task* — PR merged; =* Result= filled; Status row "Last touched"
  matches the close date.
- *Story* — Every task is =DONE= / =ABANDONED= / carried; Acceptance
  re-read and still holds.
- *Sprint* — Every story is closed; Retrospective filled; release
  notes generated if applicable.
- *Version* — Definition of done met; final sprint closed; release
  cut.
