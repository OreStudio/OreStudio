#+title: Release notes Sprint duration uses carried-over story dates
#+description: generate_release_notes.py computes Sprint duration as min/max of every story's Start/End, including carried-over stories from earlier sprints, producing a nonsensical date range.
#+type: capture
#+level: cross
#+filetags: :compass:release_notes:tooling:bug:
#+created: 2026-07-20
#+updated: 2026-07-20

This page is a [[id:671F18E4-E09C-4B3B-BD24-D33DF8AE38A6][capture]] in the *inbox* bucket of the product backlog — a pre-sprint idea, not yet pulled into a sprint as a story.

* What

=build/scripts/generate_release_notes.py= (=build_context()=, around
line 226-232) computes the release notes' "Sprint duration" line as
=min()=/=max()= over every story's =Start=/=End= dates in the sprint's
Stories table. Stories carried forward from an earlier sprint keep
their original =Start= date (that's the correct, honest record for
the story itself), so a carried-over story pulls the computed sprint
start arbitrarily far back — e.g. sprint 23's release notes reported
"Sprint duration: 2026-06-05 → 2026-07-20" because =Consolidate
history dialogs onto HistoryDialogBase= was carried from sprint 19
(created 2026-06-05), even though sprint 23 itself started
2026-07-11. Fix: read the sprint's own =#+start_date=/=#+end_date= (or
the Status table's =Start=/=End (expected)= fields) from =sprint.org=
instead of deriving it from story-level dates.

* Why

The release notes are the external-facing summary of a sprint; a
wildly wrong duration (6 weeks instead of ~9 days here) undermines
trust in the rest of the document and has to be caught and manually
corrected on every sprint close until fixed at the source.
