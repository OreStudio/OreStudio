#+description: Day-to-day account management in OreStudio: the Accounts window as system and tenant administrator, the account detail dialog and its tabs, creating accounts, and the role and permission model.

This chapter covers day-to-day account management: the Accounts window
and how it differs between administrator identities, the account detail
dialog and each of its tabs, the flow for creating a new account, and
the roles and permissions that govern what an account may do.

** Overview

@@latex:\lettrine[lines=2,nindent=0em]{E}{very} \textsc{person}@@@@html:Every person@@ and process that talks to OreStudio does so
through an /account/. The provisioning wizards described in the
[[id:A9F2C3E1-74B5-4D8A-9C6E-3B1F7D2E5A04][Initial Setup]] chapter create the first accounts for you — the
platform administrator during system provisioning and the tenant
administrator during tenant creation. Those wizards are a one-time
bootstrap: every account after that is created and managed in the
Accounts window described here.

Accounts come in four types, shown as coloured badges throughout the
UI: /User/ for people, /Service/ for OreStudio's own backend
services, /Algorithm/ for automated processes, and /LLM/ for large
language model agents. What an account is allowed to do is determined
by the /roles/ assigned to it; which data it can see is determined by
the /tenant/ it belongs to and the /parties/ it is assigned (see
[[id:A9F2C3E1-74B5-4D8A-9C6E-3B1F7D2E5A04][Initial Setup]] for the tenant and party model).

** The Accounts window

Open the Accounts window from /System ▸ Administration ▸ Accounts/.
The Administration menu is only present for accounts that hold
administrative permissions.

The window lists every account visible to your login identity, one row
per account. Alongside the username and email, badge columns show the
account type, the login status (/Online/, /Recent/, /Old/, or /Never/
for accounts that have never logged in), and whether the account is
locked. The remaining columns carry the record version and provenance —
who last modified the record and when.

The toolbar follows the standard entity-window layout (/Reload/,
/Add/, /Edit/, /Delete/, /History/) and adds four account-specific
actions: /Lock/ and /Unlock/ to suspend and restore login access,
/Reset Pwd/ to set a new password, and /Sessions/ to inspect the
account's login sessions.

*** What you see depends on who you are

Account visibility follows tenant isolation. Logged in as the platform
administrator on the system tenant, the window shows the full
machinery of the platform — the =super_admin= account plus the service
accounts that OreStudio's backend components use to talk to each
other:

#+caption[The Accounts window as system administrator]: The Accounts window as seen by the platform administrator. Most rows are service accounts — one per backend component — shown with the teal /Service/ type badge. Note the gray /Never/ login-status badges: service accounts authenticate with certificates rather than interactive logins.
[[../../../assets/images/accounts_as_system_admin.png]]

Logged in as a tenant administrator, the same window shows only the
accounts belonging to that tenant — a freshly provisioned tenant
contains exactly one, the tenant administrator itself:

#+caption[The Accounts window as tenant administrator]: The same window as seen by a tenant administrator immediately after tenant provisioning. Only the tenant's own accounts are visible — here just =tenant_admin=, currently /Online/.
[[../../../assets/images/accounts_main_window_tenant_admin.png]]

*** Live updates

Like all OreStudio entity windows, the Accounts list updates in
response to changes made elsewhere — another administrator creating an
account in a different session, for example. When unseen changes are
pending, the /Reload/ button carries a yellow stale indicator;
recently changed rows are highlighted until you have seen them.

#+caption[The stale indicator]: The Reload button showing the yellow stale indicator: the account list has changed since it was last loaded.
[[../../../assets/images/account_main_window_events.png]]

#+caption[Recently changed rows]: Newly created accounts highlighted in the list. The =ana_santos= row was recorded "just now" and remains highlighted until acknowledged.
[[../../../assets/images/account_new_account_highlighted.png]]

** The account detail dialog

Double-click an account (or select it and press /Edit/) to open the
detail dialog. The dialog has six tabs; the same dialog serves
creation, editing, and read-only inspection of historical versions.

#+caption[Account details — General tab]: The General tab for =super_admin=, showing the username, email address, and account type.
[[../../../assets/images/account_details.png]]

- /General/ — username, email, and the account type. The type is
  chosen at creation time and is read-only afterwards.
- /Security/ — set or change the account password (see the
  new-account flow below).
- /Login Status/ — read-only login telemetry: whether the account is
  online, locked, its failed login count, last login time, and last
  known IP addresses.
- /Roles/ — the roles assigned to this account (next section).
- /Parties/ — the parties this account may log in to.
- /Provenance/ — who changed this record version, when, and why.

*** Roles and parties tabs

#+caption[Account details — Roles tab]: The Roles tab for =super_admin=, holding the single =SuperAdmin= role. The combo box below the list adds further roles; the buttons alongside add and remove the selection.
[[../../../assets/images/account_details_roles.png]]

#+caption[Account details — Parties tab]: The Parties tab for =super_admin=, assigned to the internal System Party. Party assignment determines which party contexts the account may select at login.
[[../../../assets/images/account_details_parties.png]]

An account with no party assignment cannot log in to any party
context — the dialog warns you if you try to save one (see the
new-account flow below). The party-at-login mechanics are described in
[[id:A9F2C3E1-74B5-4D8A-9C6E-3B1F7D2E5A04][Initial Setup]].

*** Provenance

#+caption[Account details — Provenance tab]: The Provenance tab for =super_admin= at version 1: modified by =super_admin=, performed by the IAM service, with the =system.initial_load= change reason and the bootstrap commentary.
[[../../../assets/images/account_details_provenance.png]]

Every account record is versioned and carries full provenance,
following the same conventions as all OreStudio entities — see the
[[id:F7F8D811-44E7-4EF7-8BFF-2D96C1F580E7][Provenance]] section of the Reference Data chapter. Note the
distinction visible in the screenshot: /Modified By/ is the account on
whose behalf the change was made, while /Performed By/ records the
backend service that executed it.

** Creating an account

Press /Add/ in the Accounts window toolbar to open the same detail
dialog in creation mode, titled /New Account/.

#+caption[New Account — General tab]: Creating =john_smith=: username, email, and account type on the General tab.
[[../../../assets/images/account_new_account.png]]

Passwords are set on the /Security/ tab. The confirmation field
outlines green once both entries match; tick /Show passwords/ to
verify what you typed before saving.

#+caption[New Account — Security tab]: Setting the initial password. The green outline on the confirmation field indicates the two entries match.
[[../../../assets/images/account_new_account_security.png]]

#+caption[New Account — Show passwords]: The same tab with /Show passwords/ ticked, revealing the password text for visual verification.
[[../../../assets/images/account_new_account_see_password.png]]

Assign at least one role and, for accounts that should log in to a
party context, at least one party:

#+caption[New Account — Roles tab]: Assigning the =Trading= role to the new account. The combo box offers every role defined in the tenant.
[[../../../assets/images/account_new_account_roles.png]]

#+caption[New Account — Parties tab]: Assigning the new account to =BARCLAYS PLC=. The combo box lists the tenant's party hierarchy.
[[../../../assets/images/account_new_account_party.png]]

If you save without assigning a party, OreStudio asks for
confirmation — such accounts exist but cannot enter any party context
at login:

#+caption[The No Party Assigned warning]: The confirmation shown when saving an account with no party assignment.
[[../../../assets/images/account_missing_party.png]]

#+begin_note
At present there is no equivalent warning for /roles/: OreStudio
lets you save a new account with no role assignment and stays silent.
Such an account can log in but holds no permissions, so almost every
operation will be denied. Until a warning is added, double-check the
Roles tab before saving a new account.
#+end_note

** Roles and permissions

Open the Roles window from /System ▸ Administration ▸ Roles/. A role
is a named bundle of permissions; accounts acquire permissions only
through their assigned roles.

#+caption[The Roles window]: The Roles window listing the built-in roles — one per backend domain service plus the administrative and operational roles — with their permission counts and provenance.
[[../../../assets/images/roles_main_window.png]]

OreStudio seeds a standard set of roles at provisioning time: one
role per backend domain service (used by the service accounts seen
earlier), and operational roles such as =Trading= and =SuperAdmin= for
human users. Roles are inspected through a read-only detail dialog:

#+caption[Role details — General tab]: The =AnalyticsService= role: name and description.
[[../../../assets/images/roles_details_general.png]]

#+caption[Role details — Permissions tab]: The role's two permissions. Permission strings follow a =domain::resource:action= pattern; =analytics::*= grants every analytics permission, while =iam::tenants:read= grants read-only access to tenant records.
[[../../../assets/images/roles_details_permissions.png]]

#+caption[Role details — Provenance tab]: Role records carry the same versioned provenance as every other entity.
[[../../../assets/images/roles_details_provenance.png]]
