* What

Investigate [[https://github.com/sitmo/cgmm][sitmo/cgmm]], a Python library implementing Conditional Gaussian
Mixture Models (GMMs) with full scikit-learn compatibility, as a candidate to
port (in concept or algorithm) into our C++ synthetic market data generation
pipeline (=ores.synthetic=). The library fits a standard =GaussianMixture= on
data, then "conditions" on a subset of variables to obtain the posterior
distribution of the remaining ones. Key pieces: =GMMConditioner= (turns a
fitted mixture into a conditional mixture over target variables given
conditioning indices) and =ConditionalGMMRegressor= (an sklearn-compatible
regressor exposing =.predict()= and =.predict_cov()=, supporting multimodal
posteriors rather than single point estimates). BSD-3-Clause licensed.
Relevant capabilities highlighted in its docs: multimodal regression with
predictive confidence bands, stochastic scenario simulation/forecasting,
missing-value imputation, and inverse problems — it ships a VIX example doing
daily log-return modelling and "stochastic futures" simulation, which maps
closely onto our own synthetic market data / stochastic process generation
work (GMM improvements story, IR rates synthetic data generation).

* Why

Our synthetic data generator (=ores.synthetic=) already uses Gaussian Mixture
Models for market data simulation (see the sprint 22 "GMM improvements" story
and stochastic-process component work). cgmm's conditional-mixture approach —
sampling one set of variables conditioned on another — could let us simulate
correlated market observations (e.g. an FX rate conditioned on a curve
tenor, or a curve point conditioned on other tenors) more principled than
ad-hoc correlation injection, and its multimodal posterior support could
improve scenario realism over single-point conditional means. Worth an
appraisal task to determine whether the underlying maths is portable to our
C++ generator, or whether it is only useful as an algorithmic reference.
