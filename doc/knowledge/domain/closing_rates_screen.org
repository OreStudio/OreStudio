* Summary

The Closing Rates Screen is the point at which the automated End-of-Day
revaluation rate -- the rate the [[id:2E4294F1-41BD-4680-AB0A-C02706CD31CE][Ledger Sweep]] would otherwise apply
unattended to convert positions into [[id:E4FC332F-C404-4408-8E10-586FAF4ECCFC][functional currency]] -- meets human
judgement before EOD reports are generated. It shows Traders and
Finance the original sourced rate, any override already applied, and
the current live market rate side by side, so that a rate which looks
wrong or stale relative to the live market can be caught and corrected
before it propagates into every downstream EOD figure that depends on
it.

* Detail

** Reviewing before the rate propagates

Every currency's End-of-Day revaluation rate is sourced automatically
ahead of the EOD run, but an automatically-sourced rate can be stale,
misquoted, or simply out of line with a fast-moving market at the
moment EOD is about to be struck. The Closing Rates Screen exists to
put that rate in front of a human -- Trader or Finance -- before it is
baked into every book's revaluation, rather than only discovering an
error after EOD reports have already been produced and distributed
downstream.

** Three rates shown side by side

The screen presents, for each currency, the original rate as sourced,
any override a user has already applied, and the current market rate
as observed live. Showing all three together is what makes the review
actionable: a user can see at a glance whether the sourced rate has
already drifted from the market, whether someone has already
intervened, and by how much an override (existing or proposed)
differs from where the market currently sits, rather than having to
cross-reference three separate places to reconstruct the same
picture.

** Overriding the rate

Where the sourced rate is judged wrong or unsuitable, a Trader or
Finance user can override it directly on this screen. The override
then becomes the rate the EOD revaluation actually uses -- flowing
into the [[id:0CAD6D4D-604A-4FB9-A823-5231E759D760][FX Spot Revaluation types]] the Ledger Sweep applies -- in
place of the originally-sourced value, and it is this overridden rate,
not the raw sourced one, that subsequent EOD reports will reflect.
