#+title: Book Currencies

* Summary

Book currencies are the set of currencies a given [[id:130A3261-E788-4222-9BD7-371AD92982FB][book]] is permitted
to trade in: only instruments whose own currency intersects this set
may be booked into it at all, making book currencies a constraint on
what a book *can hold* rather than a currency role a position is
converted into. A book separately carries its own *Accounting
Currency* field -- not to be confused with the identically-named
older synonym for [[id:A7338353-8D2F-4784-92C0-B6E338C0D9C4][aggregation currency]] -- in which P&L adjustments
specific to that book are stored, alongside its functional, reserve,
ledger, and funding classification flags.

* Detail

** A constraint on what a book can hold

Where every other currency role documented under [[id:3407B8DF-D446-4658-8B5D-3368FFA44AD8][Currency]] answers a
question about how a *given* position is expressed, book currencies
answer a prior, gating question: is this instrument's currency even
eligible to be booked here at all? An instrument whose currency falls
outside a book's permitted set cannot be booked into it regardless of
how its [[id:4ADDDA46-21CE-4C94-9F22-F66805B13891][denominated currency]] or [[id:E4FC332F-C404-4408-8E10-586FAF4ECCFC][functional currency]] would otherwise be
resolved -- the constraint is checked before any of those roles come
into play.

** Accounting Currency as a per-book field

Distinct from its use elsewhere as an older synonym for [[id:A7338353-8D2F-4784-92C0-B6E338C0D9C4][aggregation
currency]], a book's own =Accounting Currency= is a concrete per-book
data field: the currency in which P&L adjustments specific to that
book are stored. The book data model carries this attribute alongside
its functional, reserve, ledger, and funding classification flags,
which is worth naming explicitly precisely because the term
"accounting currency" is overloaded -- the same two words mean a
book-level storage field in this context and a risk-engine-wide
convert-and-compare currency in the aggregation-currency context, and
the two must not be conflated when reading either concept back into
the other.
